Your BMI:


BMI stands for Body Mass Index.

It is a measure that calculates the ratio of a person's weight to their height. It's commonly used as a screening tool to categorize individuals into different weight categories, such as underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obese.


The formula to calculate BMI is:

BMI = weight (kg) / (height (m))^2

Or, if you prefer to use pounds and inches:

BMI = (weight (lbs) / (height (in))^2) x 703


Here's how the categories are typically interpreted:


Underweight: BMI less than 18.5

Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9

Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9

Obese: BMI 30 or greater


However, it's essential to note that BMI is a rough estimate and does not take into account factors like muscle mass, bone density, overall body composition, and distribution of fat, which are essential for a comprehensive assessment of an individual's health. Therefore, while BMI can provide a general indication of weight status, it should not be the sole determinant of an individual's health or fitness level.
